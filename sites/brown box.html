The Bown Box

The Brown Box is the first commercial video game console. Ralph Baer and Bill Harrison developed it in the 60’s in the USA.

Kind of technology

This console uses digital technology, binary code is used to transmit data. The device is powered by batteries; initially it didn't have a sound card, but in the following years it was implemented.

The Games

There are many games created for this console, some of these are tennis, soccer and submarine, that consists in a target shooting game, with one player moving a submarine along shipping lanes and the other player using their spot as a torpedo.

Price

The base version console was sold at a price of $ 100 that is equivalent to about $ 600 today, this contains six game cards and by paying $ 25 more you could get the "shotgun", a peripheral used for gun’s games.

Dimensions and characteristics

The console wasn't many bigger than the today's consoles, it had a rectangular shape and is called brown box for the wood-style coating. The controllers were really simple, they had only three knobs.

The Brown Box with controllers and the shotgun peripheral

Popularity

The console has spread mainly in the USA; in all, 350 thousand consoles were sold, from the release until 1975.

The evolution

Over the decades, consoles have received many improvements, for example the controllers with the advent of wireless technology doesn't need more the cable. Some examples of consoles created in the years are the gameboy, nintendo, wii, up to the newly announced Playstation 5.
